SEASONAL APP

The Seasonal Food Guide is an existing website and app that allows people to find what produce is in season according to time and region. By providing users extensive information on these foods as well as outsourced recipes and grocery shopping reminders, the Seasonal Food Guide is efficient in keeping people in the loop for seasonal produce. This personal project's goal is to offer a new brand identity, prioritize secondary features to create a one-stop experience, and most importantly, make sustainable practices more accessible to people.

LOGO DEVELOPMENT –

In my development, I explored case variations in unique open-source fonts and even attempted to create iconic elements with letter counters. Although the latter was scrapped for the logo, I used counters as the framework for other iconography in the brand. The logo of the brand is simple and to the point, using Tommaso and a grain texture to create an inviting and personable feel. The tagline, "Time to Eat", quite literally refers to the times to eat since this brand tackles seasonal produce and sustainable eating.



SKETCHES –

My sketches primarily explored how Seasonal's pages may be organized as well as what the transitions between the app's content may look like. Once I was able to identify these challenges freehand, I could move forward with them digitally and refine my ideas. While many pages transition into their own space, others share screen space - ex. "add to groceries" "save to collection", which had me critically consider the size and hierarchy of content.



WIREFRAMES –

The app is organized in four different categories – dashboard, meals, seasonal calendar, and grocery list. With these features, users can effectively learn about seasonal foods, organize their grocery list, and prepare sustainable meals. Upon signing up, users can select their preferences, which includes their region, favorite produce, dietary restrictions, and food allergies. With their preferences selected, all features will be customized for the user.
